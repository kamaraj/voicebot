### 2. Do you accommodate dietary restrictions?

**Answer:** Yes, we work with all dietary needs:

**Accommodations We Provide:**
- **Food Allergies:** Complete avoidance, substitutions
- **Religious Restrictions:** Halal, Kosher, Hindu, etc.
- **Medical Diets:** Gluten-free, dairy-free, diabetic
- **Vegetarian/Vegan:** Plant-based options
- **Texture Modifications:** Pureed, chopped, etc.

**Requirements:**
- Written documentation
- Doctor's note for allergies
- Specific restrictions listed
- Alternative preferences noted
- Emergency plan if severe allergy

**Example Substitutions:**
- Dairy allergy: Soy/oat/coconut milk
- Gluten-free: GF bread/ pasta
- Nut allergy: Sunbutter instead of peanut butter
- Vegan: Plant-based proteins

**Kitchen Practices:**
- Separate prep areas
- No cross-contamination
- Clear labeling
- Staff trained
- Regular reviews

### 4. What if my child is a picky eater?

**Answer:** We work compassionately with picky eaters:

**Our Approach:**
- **No Pressure:** Never forced to eat
- **Exposure:** Foods offered repeatedly
- **Modeling:** Peers eating
- **Positive:** Praise for trying
- **Family Style:** Serve themselves

**Strategies:**
- Offer same food multiple times
- Small portions (can have more)
- Make food fun and colorful
- Let them help prepare
- Celebrate small victories

**We Don't:**
- Force feed
- Make eat to play
- Shame for not eating
- Offer alternatives/short order
- Use food as reward/punishment

**Communication:**
- Daily food intake reported
- Parents can reinforce at home
- Consistent approach helps
- Patience and persistence

**When to Worry:**
- Losing weight
- Refusing all foods
- Only drinking liquids
- We'll notify you and may suggest pediatrician

### 5. How do you handle food allergies in the classroom?

**Answer:** Comprehensive allergy management system:

**Documentation:**
- Allergy action plan from doctor
- Photo posted in classroom
- Kitchen notified
- All staff trained
- Emergency meds available

**Classroom Protocols:**
- **Allergy Table:** If severe, separate eating area
- **No Sharing:** Strict no food sharing rule
- **Hand Washing:** Before/after meals
- **Wipes:** For surfaces
- **Supervision:** Close monitoring during meals

**Severe Allergies:**
- Epi-pen always accessible
- Staff trained to administer
- 911 called immediately
- Parents notified
- Incident report

**Classroom Activities:**
- Allergen-free art supplies
- Substitute items for activities
- Playdough without wheat (if needed)
- Non-food birthday celebrations

**Communication:**
- Parents notified of allergy in class
- Menu modifications shared
- Special events planned carefully

### 6. Can my child bring food from home?

**Answer:** Generally no, with some exceptions:

**Our Policy:**
- Meals provided (included in tuition)
- Prefer not to bring outside food
- Simplifies allergy management
- Ensures nutritional standards

**Exceptions Allowed:**
- **Breast milk/formula:** Always
- **Special dietary needs:** With documentation
- **Birthday treats:** Pre-approved, allergen-free
- **Cultural celebrations:** Coordinated with teacher

**If Bringing Food:**
1. Get prior approval
2. Label with child's name
3. List all ingredients
4. Must be store-bought (no homemade for sharing)
5. Nut-free (if nut-free facility)

**Birthday Treats:**
- Non-food preferred (stickers, books)
- If food: Individually wrapped, store-bought
- Allergen-free
- Approved 1 week advance

**Why This Policy:**
- Nutrition control
- Allergy safety
- Food equity (all children have same)
- Simplicity

### 7. What about babies? Do I provide formula/milk?

**Answer:** Parents provide formula/breast milk:

**What You Provide:**
- Formula (powder or ready-to-feed)
- Breast milk (labeled bottles)
- Bottles (labeled)
- Feeding instructions

**We Provide:**
- Storage (refrigerator/freezer)
- Bottle warming
- Clean bottles daily
- Tracking (amounts, times)
- **Transition foods** (when ready)

**Breast Milk Storage:**
- Clearly labeled (name, date)
- Stored in fridge (up to 4 days)
- Frozen milk accepted
- Warming per CDC guidelines

**Formula Preparation:**
- Parents' brand/type
- Mixed per instructions
- Fresh bottles each feeding
- Unused discarded after 1 hour

**Solid Foods:**
- Start when parent approves
- Age-appropriate textures
- Transition to table foods gradually
- Baby food provided (or you can send)

**Feeding Schedule:**
- Follow baby's cues
- On-demand for young infants
- Transition to meal times

### 9. What if my child doesn't finish their meal?

**Answer:** Child-led eating respected:

**Our Philosophy:**
- Children eat when hungry
- Stop when full
- Parents responsible for what/when
- Child responsible for how much

**During Meals:**
- Presented with nutritious options
- Encouraged to try (not forced)
- Can take more if hungry
- Can stop when full

**Reporting:**
- Daily sheet shows:
  - What was served
  - Approximate amount eaten
  - Seconds requested?
  - Mood during meal

**Not a Concern If:**
- Active and healthy
- Growing normally
- Eats some items
- Happy and energetic

**We'll Notify If:**
- Refuses multiple meals
- Seems lethargic
- Weight loss concerns
- Only drinking, not eating

**Tips:**
- Don't stress about each meal
- Look at weekly nutrition
- Offer variety at home
- Trust their appetite
