# 9. Staff & Qualifications FAQ

1. **What are the qualifications of your teachers?**
   All our lead teachers hold at least a Bachelor's degree in Early Childhood Education or a related field. Assistant teachers have a minimum of an Associate's degree or CDA credential. All staff undergo continuous professional development and are certified in CPR and First Aid.

2. **How do you screen your staff?**
   We conduct rigorous background checks, including FBI fingerprinting, state criminal history checks, and sex offender registry checks. We also verify references and education credentials for all employees before hiring.

3. **What is the staff-to-child ratio?**
   We maintain low ratios to ensure individual attention:
   - Infants (0-12 mo): 1:4
   - Toddlers (1-2 yrs): 1:5
   - Preschool (3-4 yrs): 1:10
   - Pre-K (4-5 yrs): 1:12

4. **Is there high staff turnover?**
   We are proud of our low turnover rate. Many of our teachers have been with us for over 5 years. We support our staff with competitive pay, benefits, and a positive work environment to ensure consistency for your child.

5. **Who stays with the children if a teacher is sick?**
   We have a pool of regular, qualified substitute teachers who are familiar with our center and children. In many cases, our director or assistant director will step in to ensure continuity of care.

6. **Do staff receive ongoing training?**
   Yes, all staff are required to complete at least 24 hours of professional development training annually, covering topics like child development, safety, curriculum planning, and special needs inclusion.

7. **How can I communicate with my child's teacher?**
   We encourage open communication. You can speak with teachers during drop-off/pick-up, message them via our parent app, or schedule a formal conference. We also provide daily digital reports for younger children.

8. **Are staff trained to handle medical emergencies?**
   Absolutely. Every staff member is certified in pediatric CPR and First Aid. We also have designated staff trained to administer medication and handle allergic reactions (EpiPen administration).

9. **Do you have a nurse on site?**
   While we do not have a full-time nurse, we have a health consultant who visits regularly. For daily medical needs, our administrative staff are trained in medication administration and illness assessment.

10. **How do you handle staff evaluations?**
    Teachers undergo performance evaluations twice a year, including classroom observations by the Director. We assess interactions with children, curriculum implementation, and safety compliance.
