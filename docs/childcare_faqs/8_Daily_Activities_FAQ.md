# Childcare Center - Daily Activities FAQ

## Frequently Asked Questions About Daily Activities and Curriculum

### 1. What does a typical day look like?

**Answer:** Structured yet flexible daily schedule varies by age:

**Infants (6-18 months):**
- Individual schedules based on each baby's needs
- On-demand feeding and napping
- Tummy time, sensory play, music
- Outdoor stroller walks
- Milestone development focus

**Toddlers (18 months - 3 years):**
- 7:30-8:30: Arrival, breakfast, free play
- 9:00-10:00: Circle time, songs, stories
- 10:00-11:00: Structured activities, outdoor play
- 11:00-12:00: Lunch
- 12:00-2:30: Nap time
- 2:30-3:30: Snack, quiet activities
- 3:30-6:00: Centers, outdoor play, pick-up

**Preschool (3-5 years):**
- 7:30-9:00: Arrival, breakfast, centers
- 9:00-9:30: Morning meeting, calendar
- 9:30-11:00: Learning activities, centers
- 11:00-11:30: Outdoor play
- 11:30-12:00: Lunch
- 12:00-2:00: Rest/quiet time
- 2:00-3:00: Art, science, special activities
- 3:00-6:00: Snack, centers, outdoor play

### 5. How do you teach reading and math?

**Answer:** Age-appropriate, playful approach:

**Literacy (Preschool):**
- **Letter Recognition:** Songs, games, activities

- **Print Awareness:** Books, environmental print
- **Writing:** Name writing, scribbling, pre-writing

**Not Formal Academics:**

- Hands-on activities

### 6. What about naptime/rest time?

**Answer:** Quiet rest period for all ages:

**Infants:**
- Individual nap schedules
- Follow home routine when possible
- Sleep sacks provided (or parent provides)
- Safe sleep practices (back to sleep)

**Toddlers & Preschool:**
- **Time:** 12:00-2:00 PM (approximately)
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Cots/Mats:** Provided
- **Bedding:** Parent provides (sheet, blanket, small pillow)
- **Quiet music:** Soft background

**Non-Nappers:**
- Must rest quietly for 30-45 minutes
- Then quiet activities (books, puzzles)
- Separate area if possible
- Respect sleeping children

**Transition from Napping:**
- Around age 4-5
- Gradual transition
- Communicate with parents
- Adjust rest time as needed

**Home Communication:**
- Daily sleep log for infants
- Naptime noted for older children
- Communicate sleep issues
- Adjust routine as needed

### 10. Can my child bring toys from home?

**Answer:** Limited, for specific purposes:

**Generally:**
- Prefer not to bring toys
- Plenty provided at center
- Risk of loss/damage
- Sharing difficulties

**Allowed:**
- **Nap time comfort items:** Stuffed animal, blanket
- **Show & Tell day:** Designated days only
- **Special projects:** If related to theme
- **Transitional objects:** For new/anxious children

**Not Allowed:**
- Weapons/violent toys
- Electronics/tablets
- Expensive items
- Small parts (choking hazard)
- Anything on wheels (skateboards, scooters)

**If Bringing:**
- Label with child's name
- Not responsible for damage/loss
- Child must share
- Teacher can limit if issues

**Comfort Items:**
- Can bring for nap
- Stored in cubby otherwise
- Sent home for washing regularly

**Lost Items:**
- Label everything!
- Lost & found available
- Not responsible for lost items
- Pictures of valuable items helpful
